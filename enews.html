Bloomberg (Dec 15, 2010) - Top Glove Profit Slides on Latex prices, weaker dollar.
By Barry Porter - Dec 15, 2010

Top Glove Corp., the world’s biggest rubber-glove maker, said its profit dropped for a second successive quarter in the three months ended Nov. 30 on higher latex costs and a weaker U.S. dollar.

Net income fell 45 percent to 36.1 million ringgit ($11.5 million) in its fiscal first quarter, the Malaysian company said in an exchange filing today. Average latex prices were 57 percent higher than a year ago, while the dollar was 9.3 percent weaker against the ringgit, said Top Glove, which sells its products in the U.S. currency.

“Top Glove will continue to share some of these higher costs with its customers, even though there will be a time lag for the high cost to be passed down to them,” it said.

The Selangor-based company’s shares surged 188 percent last year as global demand for medical rubber gloves outstripped supply amid a wave of global flu scares. Manufacturers, including Top Glove, responded by adding production lines as international H1N1 flu outbreaks began to ebb.

Its shares fell 1.8 percent to 5.40 ringgit at 3:52 p.m. in Kuala Lumpur today. The company released its earnings during the midday trading break.

Sales climbed 4 percent during the quarter to 491.5 million ringgit. Demand has been “normalizing with the excess capacity situation,” the statement said.

The company reported a 21 percent drop in profit to 45.1 million ringgit in its fiscal fourth quarter ended Aug. 31.
The Edge FD (Dec 15, 2010) - Top Glove 1Q earnings down 44% to RM36.05m.
Written by Joseph Chin of theedgemalaysia.com

Wednesday, 15 December 2010 14:07

KUALA LUMPUR: TOP GLOVE CORPORATION BHD’s earnings fell 44% to RM36.05 million for the first quarter ended Nov 30 from RM65.21 million a year ago as it was impacted by the high latex prices and a weak US dollar.

It said on Wednesday, Dec 13 revenue rose 4% to RM491.51 million from RM472.30 million while earnings per share were 5.83 sen compared with 10.76 sen.

“The three months to Nov 30, 2010 has been a challenging quarter for the group due to headwinds such as persistently high latex prices and the continued weakening of the US dollar coupled with the time lag in passing on the higher costs to its customers, which have affected the group’s profit margins,” it said.

Top Glove said average latex prices rose by 57% from RM4.58 per kg in 1Q2010 to RM7.20 per kg in 1Q2011 while the average US dollar weakened against the ringgit by 9.3% (RM3.43 in 1Q2010 versus RM3.11 in 1Q2011).

The world’s largest glove manufacturer said demand for rubber gloves, which has been normalising coupled with the excess capacity situation, have also impacted the industry.

It also said customers kept their inventory level at a minimum level due to high selling prices of latex gloves, which reflected the increasing cost of latex prices.

Business Times (Dec 7, 2010) - MRB: Rubber sector to stay buoyant.
By Ooi Tee Ching
bt@nstp.com.my
2010/12/07

The natural rubber (NR) industry, after a rally this year, can expect another buoyant year in 2011, says the Malaysian Rubber Board (MRB).

Tight global supply, declining stocks and seemingly unstoppable growth of China and India point towards a buoyant 2011 for the industry, MRB said.

The price of SMR (Standard Malaysian Rubber) 20 was at a new historic high of RM13.35 per kg on November 11 this year.

China and India will continue to be the main focus for the NR market as their automotive industries are poised to register double-digit growth, at least in the next two years, MRB said in a statement yesterday.

The board said the rally in 2010 had been bullish due principally to tight supplies, speculative rubber futures, impact of Thailand's imposition of a new cess effective October 1 and dwindling stocks in major producing and consuming countries.

"In recent months, output has been affected by abnormal rainfall. Local prices are also boosted by the strengthening of the ringgit."

MRB said worldwide stock levels were at all-time low and Malaysia's rubber stockpile was equivalent to five weeks of world demand, compared to 10 weeks in the past.

"Contributing factors include abnormally heavy rainfall and flood affecting producing countries."

MRB said the seasonal lull of wintering from January to March is set to reduce the country's output further by up to 40 per cent.

Rubber planting and tapping are seen a useful strategy to alleviate poverty among rural folks and bridge the income level gap with urban dwellers.

As much as 95 per cent of land planted with rubber trees in Malaysia is tapped by smallholders.

MRB said almost half of smallholders in Malaysia are solely dependent on rubber tapping for their income.

The board noted that higher rubber prices in the international markets had resulted in smallholders securing monthly income of RM2,000 to RM3,000.

High rubber prices, since 2006, also have prompted hijackings of latex-filled tankers and theft of cup lumps.

MRB acknowledged that thefts are more rampant in times of high rubber prices.

The regulator has warned the industry not to take in latex that is suspected to be hijacked or stolen.

It said it will suspend or cancel licences issued if persons caught were found to be guilty.

It is common knowledge that rising rubber prices tempt suppliers to default on lower-priced contracts.

MRB has also warned rubber exporters not to defer shipment or cancel contracts.

Top Glove Looking At Possible Acquisitions Regionally

KUALA LUMPUR, June 24 (Bernama) -- Top Glove Corporation Bhd, the world's largest rubber glove manufacturer, is looking at possible acquisitions in Indonesia, Thailand and Malaysia.

Its Chairman, Tan Sri Lim Wee Chai, said the company was currently in talks with potential parties and expected deals to be concluded or finalised within a year.

"As of now, we have committed RM80 million for ongoing expansion in new factories and production lines in Malaysia and Thailand to boost our capacity from 33 billion pieces per annum to 41.25 billion pieces per annum by 2011," Lim said during an analysts' briefing on Thursday.

Currently, Top Glove has 20 manufacturing facilities spread over Malaysia, Thailand and China.

Lim said Top Glove was bullish of its financial performance in the current financial year ending August 31, 2010 given the growing global demand for rubber gloves due to the H1N1 epidemic.

"Net profit may surge between 70 per cent and 80 per cent during that period.

"Overall, we have maintained an average profit margin of eight to 10 per cent over the last 10 years.

"For this year and 2011, we are confident of maintaining these numbers," Lim added.

As for profit after tax, Top Glove had achieved an annual compound growth rate of 36 per cent since 2001.

"We are happy if we can achieve at least 20 per cent growth rate, moving forward in the coming years," he said.

Top Glove chalked up a pre-tax profit of RM263.9 million, up 85 per cent for the nine-months ended May 31, 2010, against RM142.5 million achieved during the corresponding period.

Lim said the nine-month period was challenging for Top Glove as latex prices had surged from a low of RM4.97 per kg, in November 2009, to RM7.20 per kg, as at June 18, 2010.

Business Time (Wednesday June 17, 2010) - Top Glove Q3 profit jumps 53pc.

Published: 2010/06/17

Top Glove Corp Bhd (7113), the world's biggest rubber glove maker, continued its strong run as third quarter net profit jumped 53 per cent thanks to resilient demand.

Malaysia's rubber glove producers have been enjoying good profits since influenza A (H1N1) was declared a pandemic last year.

" ... the group is confident of continuous growth and good profitable performance in this financial year ended August 31 2010," it said in a statement to Bursa Malaysia yesterday.

Top Glove's net profit for the quarter to May 31 2010 was RM64.48 million against RM42.2 million. Revenue jumped 49 per cent to RM555.9 million.

The rubber glove maker said it is experiencing strong profit growth momentum as demand for gloves continues to increase, especially from the emerging markets.
In the first nine months to May 2010, its net profit jumped 78 per cent to RM200.22 million from a year ago. Revenue for the nine months rose 39 per cent to RM1.54 billion from RM1.11 billion a year ago.

Its balance sheet strengthened further with a net cash position of RM273 million and free cash flow of RM147.2 million for the period ended May 31 2010. It also fully redeemed some RM35 million bonds before its maturity in December 2010.

During the quarter, 1.37 million treasury shares were sold in the open market for RM17.28 million. Subsequent to May 31, a total of 5.25 million treasury shares were disposed for RM64.49 million.

Top Glove has done well despite latex prices spiking 24 per cent and the US dollar weakening by 3 per cent.

This year, it expects to return 40 per cent of its profits to investors.

Top Glove currently has 20 factories, 371 production lines with production capacity of 33 billion pieces of gloves per year and 10,900 employees. It has more than 900 customers worldwide and exports to more than 180 countries.

Top Glove Pre-tax Profit Up 85 Per Cent To RM263.9 Million

KUALA LUMPUR, June 16 (Bernama) -- Top Glove Corp Bhd's pre-tax profit for nine months ended May 31, 2010 rose by 85 per cent to RM263.9 million from RM142.5 million in the same period of 2009.

Revenue rose by 39 per cent to RM1.54 billion from RM1.10 billion in the same period last year, it said in a statement here Wednesday.

Top Glove said the strong profit growth momentum was due to the increase in demand for gloves, especially from the emerging markets.

"The company also benefited from cost-saving measures implemented at all factories, improvements in product quality, productivity, as well as aggressive marketing strategies to maintain its world number one market position," it said.

The company said it was optimistic of its outlook despite the increase in raw material costs and the weakening of US dollar.

"With a large customer base in over 180 countries, a diversified range of good quality products and a dedicated team, the group is confident of continuous growth and a profitable performance for financial year ended Aug 31, 2010," it said.

TOP GLOVE NEWS FROM BT, SOURCE IS FROM BLOOMBERG.

Published: 2010/01/21

Top Glove Bhd, the world’s largest rubber-glove maker, aims to match last year’s dividend payment of 40 per cent of its profit.

“We have better profit, good cash flow and a very healthy balance sheet,” Chairman Lim Wee Chai said in an interview in Kuala Lumpur today.

The Malaysian company raised its dividend distribution ratio to 40 per cent last year from 30 per cent as profits surged and the global flu outbreak bolstered demand for its disposable gloves.

Its profit has climbed by an average annual compounded annual rate of 36 percent in the past 15 years, Lim said. Net income gained 54 per cent to RM169.1 million in 2009.

Top Glove aims to achieve analysts’ consensus net profit forecast of RM250 million this year, or a 48 per cent increase, Lim said.

“To maintain this, we need both organic and acquisition growth,” he said.

The manufacturer has cash holdings of RM250 million. It aims to spend 100 million ringgit of this on acquisitions and RM80 million on organic growth, Lim said, adding that the company doesn’t plan to borrow or sell debt.

Top Glove is looking at companies or factories to buy in Indonesia, Thailand and Vietnam as well as in its home Malaysian market, Lim said, adding that Top Glove hopes to make its first acquisition this year.

The stock advanced 2.6 per cent to RM11.76 at midday, set for a record.

The shares have more than doubled in the past 12 months, outpacing the 50 per cent increase in the benchmark FTSE Bursa Malaysia KLCI Index over the past 12 months, surging 181 per cent.

“We have earned it,” Lim said, referring to the share price surge. - Bloomberg
